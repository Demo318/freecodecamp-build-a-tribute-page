Here are some details about Lindbergh's career:

  • 1902 - Charles Augustus Lindbergh born in Detroit, MI to Charles & Evangeline on February 4.
  • 1919 - New York hotel owner Raymond Orteig offers $25,000 prize for first non-stop flight between New York and Paris.
  • 1920 - Dropped out of the College of Engineering at the University of Wisconsinâ€“Madison.
  • 1922 - began flight training in Lincoln, NE.
  • 1923 -first solo flight, taking off from Souther Field in Americus, GA, a former Army flight training field.
  • 1925 - hired by the Robertson Aircraft Corporation in St. Louis, MO as chief pilot for 278-mile Contract Air Mail Route to provide service between St. Louis and Chicago.
  • 1927 - purchased Spirit of St. Louis with $18,000 from personal savings and outside investors, completed solo flight from New York to Paris on May 20-21, and awarded Medal of Honor by U.S. Congress.
  • 1928 - carried souvenir mail between Santo Domingo, R.D., Port-au-Prince, Haiti, and Havana, Cuba, in the Spirit of St. Louis.
  • 1932 - Charles, Jr. abducted from crib. Ransom money of $50,000 provided to kidnappers for falsified whereabouts.
  • 1936 - Lindbergh family moved to Europe.
  • 1940 - Became primary spokesman for America First Committee, upholding Monroe Doctrine that America had no business becoming involved in WWII.
  • 1942 -Approaches aviation companies to become consultant after attack on Pearl Harbor.
  • 1972 - Dies at age 72.
